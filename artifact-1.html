Artifact Summary

A no-code LLM application created for Machine Learning Fundamentals. The project demonstrates user-centered AI delivery: problem definition, prompt design, safety constraints, structured evaluation, and iterative testing.

Problem and Solution

Problem

Interview preparation is often generic, expensive, and feedback-free. Candidates need role-specific practice and useful feedback instead of static question lists or answers to memorize.

Solution

The coach accepts a target role, company type, and experience level; generates five tailored interview questions; and evaluates responses through four repeatable lenses: what worked, what is missing, what to add, and a stronger version for comparison.

Design Decisions

Input Contract

Role, company type, and experience level provide sufficient context to avoid generic output and guide question selection.

Output Schema

Each question set follows a fixed sequence: warm-up, two technical prompts, behavioral question, and curveball, with a hint about what an interviewer is assessing.

Feedback Schema

Four-part feedback promotes learning rather than scripts, helping candidates recognize gaps and improve their own answers.

Constraint Design

The prompt blocks medical advice, salary negotiation numbers, resume writing, and unrelated requests to preserve the product’s purpose and platform safety.

Evaluation

14 / 15

Full test-scenario passes across structured feedback, anxiety inputs, contradictory information, illegal interview questions, off-topic requests, and vague or nonsensical inputs.

Key Learning

In a no-code chatbot, the prompt and constraints determine the product’s behavior. The work reinforced that prompt engineering, safety boundaries, and scenario testing are first-class engineering concerns in production LLM systems.
