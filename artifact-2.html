Case Study

Machine Learning: Banking Fraud Detection

Fraud detection begins with structured transaction data: amount, merchant category, location, device signal, account behavior, and transaction velocity. Supervised ML models, such as logistic regression and tree-based methods, can score risk quickly while retaining useful explanations for investigators and auditors.

  • Why ML fits: tabular data, fast scoring, and feature patterns that can be engineered with domain knowledge.
  • Business value: interpretable signals support fraud operations, governance, and customer-impact decisions.
  • Why DL is less suitable: it adds training cost and complexity without a clear advantage when meaningful features already exist.

Deep Learning: Autonomous-Driving Image Recognition

Autonomous vehicles process high-dimensional visual input: road markings, pedestrians, traffic signs, vehicles, and changing weather or lighting. Deep neural networks can learn layered visual features directly from raw images in a way simple models cannot match.

  • Why DL fits: raw visual data contains complex spatial patterns and millions of input values per frame.
  • Business value: learned representations improve perception across varied real-world driving conditions.
  • Why simple ML is less suitable: manual feature engineering cannot reliably capture scene context and visual variation.
